# Syncode Visual System And Workspace Design

## Context

`Syncode` currently has the functional ingredients of a serious product system, but its interface language still leans toward a polished demo or concept surface rather than a mature, continuously used working tool. The most visible symptoms are oversized radii, card-heavy composition, decorative atmospheric backgrounds, and an AI presentation style that competes with the product instead of supporting it.

The user explicitly wants the product to move toward a blend of `Linear` and `Cursor`:

- keep `Linear`'s order, density, grid discipline, and professional restraint
- keep `Cursor`'s ability to make the product surface itself feel powerful and real
- remove the generic "AI product landing page" feel
- make the homepage inherit the product's interface language rather than inventing a separate marketing shell

This spec defines the global visual system first, then anchors the first implementation round on the `workspace` page inside the app. The homepage is intentionally deferred until the app itself produces screenshot-worthy product surfaces.

## Product-Level Design Goal

Transform `Syncode` from:

- large-radius, floating, card-driven, concept-heavy interface presentation

into:

- compact, precise, professional, screenshot-ready product workbench surfaces

The guiding principle is:

> `Syncode` should feel like a real system people work inside every day, not a product being presented from the outside.

## Desired Product Character

### Core traits

- Professional
- Precise
- Dense but readable
- Quietly confident
- Product-first
- Screenshotable

### Explicitly avoid

- heavy AI glow aesthetics
- oversized rounded cards
- excessive floating panels
- soft "friendly SaaS" module grammar
- homepage-first polish that breaks once users enter the product

## Global Visual System

### 1. Shape language

The entire product needs a tighter radius hierarchy.

Target direction:

- primary shell radius: `12px`
- secondary panel radius: `10px`
- controls radius: `8px`
- pills remain fully rounded only where they function as tags or state chips

Design rule:

- radius is a finishing tool, not the main visual personality of the product

Consequences:

- remove or reduce all large `20px+` panel radii on major surfaces
- avoid stacking large rounded shells inside large rounded shells
- reserve softer rounding only for intentionally small, local elements

### 2. Panel language

`Syncode` currently overuses panel wrappers as independent visual objects. The new system should weaken outer panel identity and strengthen structural relationships.

Target direction:

- fewer standalone "cards"
- more continuous shells
- clearer internal separators
- more reliance on sectioning, columns, and tool rows

Design rule:

- a surface should read as part of a work system before it reads as a component

### 3. Borders and separators

Borders become more important than shadow in the new design system.

Target direction:

- subtle but present outer border for major shells
- consistent vertical and horizontal separators for structural logic
- fewer fully boxed inner blocks
- more use of section division and inset grouping

Design rule:

- use borders to reveal structure, not to give every block isolated presence

### 4. Shadows

Shadows should retreat significantly.

Target direction:

- minimal or near-absent inner surface shadows
- only a light workspace-level elevation if needed
- hover states should rely more on border/background shifts than vertical lift

Design rule:

- if a shadow makes a panel feel like it is floating, it is probably too strong

### 5. Backgrounds and atmospheric treatment

The current background system uses too much decorative atmosphere for a professional product shell.

Target direction:

- quieter app-level background
- lower reliance on radial glow, haze, and ambient gradients
- stronger contrast generated by layout and surface hierarchy rather than background effects

Design rule:

- the content and structure should build depth; the background should not perform that job

### 6. Color usage

Accent color should become more intentional and less ambient.

Target direction:

- use brand/accent color for actions, key state, and a few controlled focus moments
- reduce accent as full-page atmosphere
- keep most product surfaces neutral and stable

Design rule:

- accent should signal importance, not decorate the whole screen

### 7. Typography and density

The product should become slightly denser and more system-like.

Target direction:

- tighter section spacing
- less empty air between modules
- stronger hierarchy through size, weight, and spacing rather than effects
- cleaner uppercase or micro-label usage where helpful

Design rule:

- density should communicate competence, but not at the cost of comprehension

## Homepage Role In The System

The homepage is not a separate aesthetic layer. It should be the public-facing expression of the product system.

That means:

- the homepage must inherit the same radius, border, density, and screenshot logic as the app
- the homepage should rely on real product surfaces, not synthetic fake dashboards
- the homepage should feel like the product opened outward, not like a marketing page painted inward

This spec intentionally defers homepage implementation until the app's key mother template is corrected.

## Mother Template Choice: Workspace

The first page to redesign should be the app workspace:

- problem context
- editor workbench
- judge/result behavior
- AI assistant rail

Reason:

- it contains the strongest expression of the product's differentiated value
- it is the most likely future homepage screenshot source
- once this page feels right, other app pages and the homepage can inherit from it

## Workspace Design Direction

### Current problem

The workspace already has the correct functional pieces, but its visual organization still reads as:

- left card
- center card
- right card

instead of:

- one continuous work system with three functional zones

### Chosen direction

Use the `Continuous Workbench` model.

This means:

- one main shell for the working environment
- problem, editor, and AI defined by internal structure rather than separate oversized shells
- center editor as the visual gravity center
- AI as a native side tool, not an attached chat widget
- problem panel as current-task context, not an article-reading page

## Workspace Structural Rules

### 1. Single main shell

The workspace should visually read as one workbench first.

Required outcome:

- reduce the current "three large framed columns" feeling
- move emphasis from outer wrappers to internal organization

### 2. Editor as gravity center

The editor must carry the strongest visual weight.

Required outcome:

- center column gets the clearest functional priority
- more usable area is given to code and code-adjacent tools
- surrounding zones support the editor rather than compete with it

### 3. Problem panel as task context

The left side should stop behaving like a full reading page.

Required outcome:

- top area quickly communicates title, difficulty, tags, and limits
- the rest is organized as concise task context: description, examples, hints, and result access
- visual rhythm favors operational clarity over decorative content framing

### 4. AI as professional side tool

The right side should stop resembling a friendly messenger or generic AI chat panel.

Required outcome:

- stronger relationship to current question, code, and judge state
- lower "conversation product" energy
- more tool-like organization for prompts, outputs, and quick actions

### 5. Continuous substructure

The internal layout should rely on:

- tool rows
- tabs
- section separators
- restrained inset blocks

instead of:

- stacked independent sub-cards with repeated heavy framing

## Workspace Visual Parameters

### Shell and column hierarchy

Target direction:

- outer workspace shell: restrained
- internal column division: explicit
- each column can contain sections, but should not itself become a giant card object

### Suggested width distribution

Baseline target:

- left problem context: `32% - 36%`
- center editor: `42% - 48%`
- right AI rail: `20% - 24%`

Design rule:

- give additional width to the editor before giving it to AI

### Radius hierarchy

Target direction:

- workspace shell: `12px`
- internal panels and sections: `10px`
- controls and compact tools: `8px`

### Border logic

Required persistence:

- preserve the overall workspace boundary
- preserve clear column separators
- preserve tool-row separators

Required reduction:

- reduce unnecessary borders around every inner content block

### Shadow logic

Target direction:

- major reduction in shadow dependence
- no panel should feel like it is floating off the work surface

### Background logic

Target direction:

- calmer, darker, more stable workspace background
- editor and work surfaces build depth
- decorative atmosphere retreats

## App-Specific Refactoring Intent

The first implementation round should not attempt a full product redesign. It should intentionally change only what is needed to establish the new system.

### In scope for round one

1. Shared tokens and base UI primitives that currently encode the wrong visual grammar
2. Workspace shell and layout composition
3. Problem-side visual organization
4. Editor-side workbench styling and relationships
5. AI-side visual reframing as a side tool

### Explicitly out of scope for round one

1. Full homepage redesign
2. Full dashboard redesign
3. Full admin redesign
4. New feature scope
5. Broad product copy rewrite

## Required First-Round Outcomes

Round one is successful only if all of the following become true:

1. The workspace clearly reads as one continuous workbench, not three large cards
2. Radius, border, density, and shadow behavior visibly shift toward a more mature product language
3. The AI panel feels integrated and professional rather than chat-first
4. The page can produce credible homepage-grade screenshots
5. The visual system is strong enough to drive future homepage and dashboard work without rethinking fundamentals again

## Design Risks

### Risk 1: overcorrecting into sterility

If the redesign becomes too strict or flattened, `Syncode` may lose product energy.

Mitigation:

- keep screenshot impact and editor prominence
- use contrast and composition, not decoration, for energy

### Risk 2: preserving too much card grammar

If large shells and nested boxes remain, the whole redesign will feel superficial.

Mitigation:

- prioritize shell simplification over surface decoration

### Risk 3: AI still dominating product perception

If the AI rail still reads as a chat product, the app will continue feeling "AI flavored" instead of product-first.

Mitigation:

- enforce contextual, tool-like presentation for AI surfaces

### Risk 4: homepage and app drifting apart later

If the homepage is designed independently afterward, the product may split visually again.

Mitigation:

- treat the workspace as the visual source of truth
- build homepage around real product captures from the corrected app

## Decision Summary

The accepted direction is:

- `Linear + Cursor`
- more order than spectacle
- more workbench than marketing
- more structure than decoration
- homepage follows product
- workspace becomes the mother template first

The first implementation round should therefore begin with:

1. shared visual tokens and primitives
2. workspace shell and internal structure
3. problem/editor/AI rebalance

Only after that should homepage design move forward using real product screenshots from the corrected workspace.
